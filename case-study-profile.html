Overview

A digital resume with personality

The personal profile site serves as my digital resume and the central hub for my professional work. My main goal was to communicate my unique career journey. I wanted to show hiring managers and design peers how my background in education and digital humanities makes me a better frontend developer.

Instead of using a generic template, I designed this site from scratch to reflect my own organized mindset. It is not just a list of my skills. It is a practical demonstration of my ability to structure information logically and present it beautifully.

Design Decisions

Designing for yourself

Designing for yourself is surprisingly hard because there are no external constraints or client briefs to guide you. I ultimately chose a dark visual direction with subtle warm accents. This creates a calm, focused, and professional atmosphere that matches my working style.

To balance personality with professionalism, I avoided overly flashy elements. I focused heavily on high-quality typography and clear information hierarchy. I also used data visualization, such as custom radar charts, to display my skill sets creatively rather than relying on standard bullet points.

Technical Approach

Under the hood

I built this project without any heavy frameworks to ensure optimal loading speeds. The most interesting technical challenge was implementing the smooth scroll-reveal animations across the different sections.

Instead of using a third-party animation library, I decided to use the native Intersection Observer API in vanilla JavaScript. This was my first time relying entirely on this API. It allowed me to trigger CSS animations exactly when elements enter the viewport, resulting in a highly performant and elegant browsing experience.

I also pushed my CSS Grid skills further by creating a complex, responsive layout for the resume section that adapts perfectly to mobile screens without breaking the reading flow.

Reflection

What I took away

01 / Learned
Content strategy is crucial. I learned that writing clear and concise copy about my own background takes just as much effort as building the layouts. Good design cannot fix bad content.
02 / Would Do Differently
Plan the CSS architecture earlier. I ended up rewriting several CSS classes halfway through the project because I did not plan my global variables properly at the start. Next time, I will establish a strict design token system from day one.
03 / Proud Of
The custom skill visualizations. I am very proud of the interactive charts used to display my skills. It breaks away from standard resume formats and perfectly bridges my design thinking with my technical execution.
