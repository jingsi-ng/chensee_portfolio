Overview

Exploring visual communication

The brief for this Visual Communication assignment was to design two separate posters exploring different themes. We were tasked with creating one music-related poster and one film-related poster.

For the music theme, I chose to create a vintage tribute concert poster for Frank Sinatra. For the film theme, I designed a modern sci-fi movie poster for the classic geometric satire, Flatland. My main goal was to demonstrate how different design languages and typographic choices can completely change the mood of a visual piece.

The Designs
Poster 01 / Music Theme
Frank Sinatra Tribute Poster. I wanted to capture the timeless and smooth atmosphere of a 1960s jazz concert. I used warm gradients and intersecting circular text elements to represent the rhythm of the music and the spinning motion of a vinyl record.
Poster 02 / Film Theme
Flatland Sci-Fi Movie Poster. Based on Edwin A. Abbott's famous novel, this poster explores the concept of a dimensional breach. I focused on strict geometric layouts and mysterious visual tension to highlight the mathematical and satirical nature of the story.
Design Process

How I approached each poster

I started by researching the visual history of both genres. For the Sinatra poster, I studied 1960s jazz album covers and classic concert flyers to understand their use of space and color. For Flatland, I analyzed modern minimalist sci-fi movie posters to see how they build tension with minimal elements.

Before opening Photoshop, I sketched several layout options on paper. This helped me lock in the visual hierarchy and compositional balance. I always ensure the core concept is strong on paper before moving to digital execution.

Typography & Colour

Visual decisions

For the music poster, I used an elegant serif font with a glowing effect for the main title to create a timeless and classic feel. I paired this with warm orange and deep brown gradients to reflect the intimate, sophisticated atmosphere of a live jazz club.

For the Flatland poster, I chose sharp and structured typography to emphasize the mathematical themes of the story. The restricted color palette and stark contrast help to highlight the mysterious warning of a "dimensional breach", making the poster feel cinematic and urgent.

Reflection

What I took away

01 / Learned
Advanced Photoshop composition. I learned how to effectively use blending modes, gradient maps, and layer masks to create rich and textured backgrounds instead of relying on flat colors. This significantly improved my overall visual design skills.
02 / Would Do Differently
Experiment more with custom shapes. If I were to design these again, I would spend more time creating custom geometric shapes or hand-drawn typographic elements from scratch to make the compositions even more unique and personalized.
03 / Proud Of
The circular text layout. I am very proud of the intersecting circular text on the Sinatra poster. It required precise alignment and path manipulation in Photoshop. It successfully acts as a visual focal point that mimics a spinning vinyl record.
